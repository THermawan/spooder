Glossary

Confused? Don't know what all these big words mean? Never fear, the glossary's got you covered!

Arteries and Veins

Have you ever seen a road map? If you have, you've probably noticed there are roads going everywhere. Your body, too, has a highway system that sends blood to and from different body parts. This system is called the circulatory system, and those roads, like in a map, are called the arteries and veins. Arteries, which usually look red, carry blood and nutrients away from the heart. Veins, which usually look blue, return blood to the heart, ready to be filled with oxygen and nutrients before being pumped out again through arteries.


Blockage

Something that stops something (such as blood) from moving through something (such as a blood vessel).


Blood Clot

Have you ever put water into the freezer then taken it out and seeing that it turns into ice? We can say that the water went from being a liquid to becoming a solid. Just like the water, out blood clots when it changes from a liquid form to a thickened or solid state.


Blood Pressure

The pressure of the flowing blood against the walls of the blood vessels.


Brain

An organ inside your head that controls and regulate everything in your body, even when you're asleep! This thing keeps you alive.


Burst

To break open or into pieces in a sudden and violent way.


Cells

All living things are made of cells. These are the smallest building blocks of every living thing on our planet, including humans like you and me. The trillions of cells in out bodies do everything from changing your food into energy to keeping your body healthy.


Cholesterol

A type of fat found in your blood. Your liver makes cholesterol for your body. You also can get cholesterol from the foods you eat. Meat, fish, eggs, butter, cheese, and whole or low-fat milk all have cholesterol in them.


CT Scan (CAT Scan)

If you are wondering or have heard of a CAT scan, it's funny name suggests that it involves pet cats. Funnily enough, a CT scan, which people like to say as 'CAT scan', actually has nothing to with cats. The CT (short for computed tomography) is a kind of x-ray which gives a doctor a clearer picture of what is happening inside the body. The pain free scan shows bones and other parts of the body in detail.


Disease

A disease is any abnormal (not normal) condition of the body or mind that causes discomfort or dysfunction.


Fever

A fever is the rising of the body's temperature. It's part of your body's response against infection.


High Blood Pressure

A common disorder where blood pressure is unreasonably high. Not healthy. Usually happens because too much sodium (salt) has been consumed.


Infection

When germs get inside your body, they can multiply and cause an infection. Your body's immune system fights off the germs with special cells. It can become a full-on fight against the nasty invaders â€“ and you won't feel better until your body wins! Sometimes when you get sick, it's because you have some kind of infection. If your body's having a little trouble fighting the infection by itself, your doctor may prescribe medicine like antibiotics to help, so just sit back, get some rest, and let your body do the work!


Intravenous (IV)

Existing or taking place within, or administered into, a vein or veins.


Numbness (Numb)

Lacking sensation; "my foot is asleep"; "numb with cold". It can also mean "to make insensitive". (e.g. the feeling you get when you have been sitting with your legs crossed for a long amount of time.)


Nutrients

Nutrients in our body work together to contribute to good health. There are six major types of nutrients which are found in our bodies: carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins, minerals and water. Each of these has a specific function to keep the body healthy.


Oxygen

Ever wondered what it is in the air we are breathing in? Well the answer is simple, oxygen! Oxygen is the most common chemical element found on Earth. It is necessary for the survival of all living things, such as plants, animals and humans.


Prescription Drugs

A drug that is available only with written instructions from a doctor or dentist to a pharmacist; "he told the doctor that he had been taking his prescription regularly".


Prevent

To keep from happening or arising.


Radiation

Radiation is the emission of energy as either waves (electromagnetic radiation) or particles (particle radiation).


Rehabilitation

The act of restoring something to its original state. This can also mean "to bring (someone or something) back to a normal, healthy condition after an illness, injury, drug problem, etc."
