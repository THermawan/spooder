Integrative Medicine II

IM and Stroke Patients

Some stroke patients might want to try other alternative treatments for stroke to add to their prescribed medicine from their doctor. Although these are not directly treating stroke, they cab help your loved one recover and prevent future strokes.

Acupuncture has been used for centuries in Asian culture. To ease pain and treat muscled problems which are affected by stroke, small needles are inserted into specific points in the body.

Diet changes may be necessary because unhealthy amounts of cholesterol in the blood can cause clotting. By making healthy eating habits, one can reach healthy cholesterol levels. Some changed may include avoiding food high in fat, or sodium/salt rich foods.

Massage is a common pain relief for many conditions. It can help people who have had stroke by helping their mood, lowering their blood pressure, easing anxiety and relaxing the body.

Yoga can help improve breathing and physical movements. It is a good exercise program that improves mental focus that may have been lost during the stroke.

Herbal Medicine can improve the circulation of blood to the brain and help it function better. Some may prevent the person from having another stroke. It is important to speak to a doctor before using herbs, as some may have allergic reactions or may not react well.
